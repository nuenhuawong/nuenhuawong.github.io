Our Approach to Tennis Training

At Tennis Learning in Singapore, we believe that effective tennis coaching must be tailored to each individual's unique needs, goals, and learning style. Our programs are designed to provide structured progression while remaining flexible enough to address your specific areas for improvement.

All programs include:

  • Personalized skill assessment and goal setting
  • Technical instruction focused on proper mechanics
  • Progressive drills that build in complexity
  • Game situation practice and strategy development
  • Regular progress evaluation and feedback
  • Off-court exercise recommendations

Beginners Program

Perfect for those new to tennis or returning after a long break. Our beginners program focuses on building a solid foundation of fundamental skills in a supportive, encouraging environment.

What You'll Learn:

  • Proper grip techniques for different strokes
  • Basic forehand and backhand groundstrokes
  • Serving mechanics and technique
  • Court positioning and basic movement patterns
  • Scoring and basic rules of the game
  • Simple rally techniques and consistency

Recommended Format:

Individual or small group sessions (2-4 people), 1-2 times per week

Intermediate Program

For players who have mastered the basics and are looking to refine their skills, develop consistency, and improve their strategic gameplay. Our intermediate program focuses on taking your tennis to the next level.

What You'll Learn:

  • Advanced stroke techniques and variations
  • Spin control and shot placement
  • Volley techniques and net play
  • Serve variations and placement strategies
  • Footwork and court coverage improvement
  • Match strategy and tactical decision-making

Recommended Format:

Individual or small group sessions (2-4 people), 1-3 times per week

Frequently Asked Questions

What equipment do I need to bring?

For beginners, we can provide racquets for your first few sessions. Eventually, you'll want your own racquet. Always bring water, a towel, and wear appropriate tennis shoes and comfortable athletic clothing.

How often should I take lessons?

For consistent improvement, we recommend at least one session per week. More frequent sessions will accelerate your progress, especially when combined with independent practice between lessons.

Can I switch between individual and group sessions?

Yes, many students benefit from a combination of both formats. Individual sessions provide focused technical development, while group sessions offer practical application in a more dynamic environment.

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancellations must be made at least 24 hours in advance for a full refund or rescheduling. Cancellations with less notice may be subject to a fee, except in cases of inclement weather or emergencies.

What happens if it rains?

If a session is stopped due to rain, the remaining minutes will be added to a future training session. We'll contact you to reschedule at a convenient time.

How long does it take to see improvement?

Most students notice improvements in their technique within 3-5 sessions. Significant progress in match play typically takes 2-3 months of regular training, depending on practice frequency and prior experience.
